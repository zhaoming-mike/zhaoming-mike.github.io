Five Vehicle Classes
The game includes five vehicle classes: Light Tank, Medium Tank, Heavy Tank, SPG (Self-Propelled Gun/Artillery), and Tank Destroyer. Each class possesses specific characteristics and a tier number; higher tier means more powerful vehicles.

Subclasses may be used to give users a quick approximation of vehicle attributes without detailed review. Premium vehicles exist as a special category with their own advantages, including access to different battle level ranges compared to non-premium vehicles of the same tier.

Character Object Model (Figure 5A)
Character object instance 501 has class 505 with attributes: Name 511, Qualification 512 (driver/gunner/etc.), Training Level 513, Competence 514 (specific vehicle proficiency), and Additional Skills 509 (Repair, Firefighting, Camouflage).

Each attribute has a value that can be increased by allocating experience points. Training Level affects gameplay performance (e.g., higher gunner training → more accurate shooting). Additional skills have percentage effectiveness. Qualification cannot be changed (a driver cannot be retrained as a gunner). Competence can be changed by retraining for a different vehicle, but this decreases Training Level in the new vehicle.

Vehicle Object & Modules (Figure 5B)
Vehicles contain module classes 559: Gun 575, Turret 576, Engine 577, Suspension 578, and Radio 579. Within each class, multiple module types are available (e.g., five increasingly powerful gun types). Upgrading modules alters vehicle attributes.

Vehicle attributes 557 include firepower, armor/shielding, speed/maneuverability, and signal range. Changing a module type alters attributes based on the new module's effectiveness. For example, upgrading the Radio module increases Signal Range, allowing detection of enemies at greater distances. Vehicle subclasses (light/medium/heavy/etc.) provide a general approximation of attributes.

Experience Points & Research
Between game sessions, new vehicles and modules are unlocked using experience points. Two experience classes may exist: one for accessing game objects, another for other features. Free experience can be converted from battle experience, with premium vehicles enabling conversion without prerequisites.

Experience points drive the progression economy. Players earn points through battles, then spend them to research new modules and vehicles via tech trees (Figures 6A, 6B). Premium vehicles allow conversion of battle experience to free experience without meeting predetermined conditions that apply to non-premium vehicles.
